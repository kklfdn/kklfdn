Meet Karlson Leung

  • Student
  • Wanderer
  • Advocate
  • Statesman

Karlson King-Tao Leung, FRSA was raised in Vancouver and educated at the University of British Columbia and the University of Oxford. Known as ‘King’ among his retinue for his regal habits and majestic comportment, he has been a passionate progressive voice throughout his time at U of T Law.

Student

As a life-long learner, Karlson has long been a man of integrity and passion. While an undergraduate student at UBC, he helped exposed an underground essay mill. He wrote his graduate thesis on authoritarian land use policy and expropriation. Yet in law school, he found himself asking: “How could this have happened?” While at times he regretted his vocational choice, your passion and energy inspired him to focus his efforts on making law school a positive place for everyone.

Wanderer

King’s passion for the public good has led him around the world, talking to and interacting with scores of average people. Through his time at the UN Development Programme in Beijing, as an intern at the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia, and as a Fellow of the Royal Society of the Arts, he became a truly global citizen. Yet, he remains deeply rooted to U of T Law. His passion for human rights resulted in his executive role with the CBA’s Constitutional and Human Rights Section and as the Editor in Chief of Rights Review.

Advocate

At a time when many were silent, Karlson took strong, principled positions on issues that mattered to students. After witnessing first-hand the stresses caused by ExamSoft, he wanted to ensure that no student would be left behind. His tireless advocacy was noted by his peers to be the most effective student-led campaign in the law school’s recent history.

“We must stop the financialization of housing.”

He has also been a progressive voice on social policy. He has harshly criticized the global financialization of housing. He was quoted in Quartz for his expertise on direct action and protests during Hong Kong’s Umbrella Revolution. He has also opined on the importance of religious accommodations.

We need exam software that works for everyone. K.K. Leung

Statesman

As a believer in building relationships with all people, King was a member of both the Conservative and Labour Associations at Oxford. He remains steadfastly non-partisan, instead focusing on the importance of coming together. He is a firm supporter of local, national, and transnational institutions.

We cannot stop until we lift every child. K.K. Leung
